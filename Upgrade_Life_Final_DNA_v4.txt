4. THE PRODUCTION ENGINE (STORY & VISUALS)
------------------------------------------
* THE SCRIPT LOOP: Every video follows a 4-act structure:
    1. THE MESS: A relatable, witty opening calling out a common life struggle [cite: 2, 3].
    2. THE DEEP DIVE: Explaining the "why" behind the struggle using human psychology and life patterns [cite: 2, 3].
    3. THE MIRROR: The moment of brutal honesty where the Big Brother points out the viewer's current habits [cite: 2, 3].
    4. THE WAY FORWARD: Presenting practical "Upgrades" to help the viewer find their way back to a secure feeling [cite: 2, 3].
* OFFICIAL VISUAL LANGUAGE (CHIBI-LITE INFOGRAPHIC COMIC) — canonical for all stills, thumbnails, and agent prompts [cite: 2, 3]:
    - CHARACTER ANATOMY (Chibi-Lite): Roughly 2.5 to 3 heads tall (soft super-deformed proportions)—friendly, non-threatening, wise. Minimalist faces: simple dot eyes and thin expressive eyebrows for subtle emotion reads. Consistent hero: brown hair, light beard stubble, signature sage-green zip hoodie; same look every frame.
    - LINE WORK AND RENDERING: Thick clean black or dark grey digital outlines, consistent stroke weight (sticker-like pop). Soft cel-shading—single-layer shadows (e.g. under chin, hoodie folds) without busy texture. Vector clarity: every element scannable at small thumbnail sizes.
    - NARRATIVE LAYERING (infographic comic): Floating white rectangular tags, speech bubbles, and status labels (e.g. "$7.49", "PAID", "CAR FUND") to tell the story visually when relevant. Backgrounds may be detailed but soft-focused so the host, props, and any labels stay readable—avoid muddy, overly busy fills.
    - LIGHTING AND CYBER-STOIC PALETTE: Atmospheric glow from lamps, sunset, or glowing bulbs—"Digital Sanctuary" feeling. Deep navy for stability; sage green on the mentor; warm amber for success and financial peace; neon cyan for clarity highlights. High contrast: bright red and orange reserved strictly for stressors (e.g. inflation alarm, debt)—draw the eye to the problem being solved.
    - STORYBOARD VIBE: Panel or single-frame energy with one clear lesson and readable focal hierarchy. Educational webcomic tone—built to teach while feeling like a late-night talk with a friend [cite: 2, 3].
* VISUAL STYLE (summary): 2D illustrated stills using the Official Visual Language above, consistent character in every frame [cite: 2].
* CINEMATOGRAPHY: Extensive use of the Ken Burns effect (slow pans and zooms) in sync with the narration [cite: 2].
* COLORS: Follow the Cyber-Stoic logic in Official Visual Language; deep navy foundation with neon cyan and warm amber highlights as described there [cite: 2, 3].
* THUMBNAIL OVERLAY (NON-NEGOTIABLE): The curiosity line (2–6 words, ALL CAPS) must always appear in the finished thumbnail—large, sharp, and readable, integrated into the graphic design (title-card / pill), never tiny or cropped away [cite: 3].
* THUMBNAIL CURIOSITY COPY: Overlay words should open a loop (why / how / what if)—tease the payoff without spoiling the whole story; avoid flat labels that could be any channel [cite: 3].
* THUMBNAIL HOST CONSISTENCY: The same recurring Big Brother in every still—Chibi-Lite proportions (2.5–3 heads tall), dot eyes and thin brows, brown hair with light beard stubble, dark blue or dark beanie, sage-green zip hoodie with brown plaid shirt collar showing, khaki pants, optional SORTED LIFE mug. Do not change proportions, face style, age read, hair, stubble, outfit, or body type between thumbnails [cite: 2, 3].
* AUDIO SIGNATURE: Dynamic, expressive vocals with a rich quality and lofi-beats or environmental textures like rain [cite: 1, 2]. Machine TTS uses the same persona via `src/prompts/vocal-dna.ts` (system instruction + Studio Audio → Gemini TTS).
